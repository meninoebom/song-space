# Commercial Angles for Song Space

Living document — brainstorming how this work becomes sustainable.

## What We Actually Have

A **real-time body-to-music mapping engine** that runs in the browser with just a webcam. Configurable framework: readings, mappings, triggers, arcs, scores. Zero-install, zero-friction. The score system is designed for extensibility and transfers into Ralf.

## Commercial Angles

### 1. Fitness / Wellness Classes (fastest to revenue)

Peloton proved people pay for music-driven movement experiences. This system could power class formats where movement drives the music — yoga where stillness deepens the soundscape, dance fitness where energy builds the mix.

- **Model:** Software subscription or license to studios/platforms
- **Buyers:** Boutique studios, ClassPass, Apple Fitness+, Mirror/Lululemon Studio
- **Advantage:** Browser-based = no hardware investment for studios
- **Next step:** Pilot a free class at 2-3 local studios, film it, get testimonials

### 2. Brand Activations / Experiential Events (lumpy but large payments)

Brands pay $50K-$500K for experiential activations (Nike, Red Bull, Spotify events). An installation where people's movement shapes the music is what experiential agencies pitch.

- **Model:** Per-project license or tech provider fee
- **Buyers:** Experiential agencies (Superfly, MKG, Momentum Worldwide), brand marketing teams
- **Advantage:** Novel, visual, shareable on social media
- **Next step:** 3-minute demo video showing movement driving music in a room. Reach out to agencies.

### 3. Music Therapy / Rehabilitation (slower, high ceiling)

Movement-to-music feedback loops are clinically studied for stroke recovery, Parkinson's, autism. Browser + webcam = massive cost advantage over clinical setups.

- **Model:** Clinical licensing, eventually insurance reimbursement
- **Buyers:** Rehabilitation centers, therapists, research institutions
- **Advantage:** NIH/NSF grants pay better than arts grants; "artist who built this" + "clinical tool" is a strong narrative
- **Next step:** Research partnerships, clinical validation studies

### 4. Music Education

Teaching rhythm, dynamics, and musical structure through movement. Orff and Dalcroze methods already do this — Song Space is the digital version.

- **Model:** SaaS, per-seat pricing for schools/programs
- **Buyers:** Schools, music programs, after-school enrichment
- **Next step:** Talk to music educators, understand curriculum fit

### 5. SDK / Body Interaction Middleware

The score system (readings/mappings/triggers) is a generalizable "body interaction engine." Useful for game developers, VR, accessibility tools, creative coders.

- **Model:** Open-source engine + paid hosted processing or premium scores
- **Buyers:** Developers, studios, accessibility orgs
- **Next step:** Extract and document the API; this happens naturally as Ralf matures

## Strategy

**Don't try all five.** Combine #1 (recurring revenue) and #2 (large project payments) first. These sustain while the art practice grows.

The gap between "working prototype" and "thing someone pays for" is closed by showing it to people in a room, not by more engineering.
